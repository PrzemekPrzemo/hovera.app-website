The Pony Club is the backbone of UK youth equestrianism — 280+ branches, 35,000+ members. If your yard hosts a branch (DC, instructor, equine resources), you're juggling: membership renewals, achievement badges, rally bookings, certificate prints, parent-volunteer rotas, fundraising. Most branches run on a single overworked DC and a few dedicated parents.

## The admin volume

A typical 80-member branch runs annually:
- 15-20 working rallies
- 8-12 fun rides / events
- 4-6 official tests (D, C, C+, B)
- 2-3 camps (residential + day)
- Membership intake / renewals (rolling)
- Finance reconciliation (membership fees, event fees, sponsorship)

That's 60-80 admin events × ~30 minutes each = **30-40 hours/year** of pure paperwork.

## Where the time goes

### Membership

Manual register, Excel spreadsheet linked to bank statements. Members miss renewals because nobody remembered to chase. Each missed renewal = £25-40 lost revenue + hassle.

### Event bookings

Email + WhatsApp + paper sign-up sheet at rallies. Names get typed twice, missed, allergies forgotten.

### Achievement records

Each badge attempt: form filled, sent to Area Office, certificate printed, given to child. Lost forms means re-take.

### Reconciliation

End-of-year branch report to area: financial, membership numbers, events held. Reconstructing from receipts and notes — full weekend.

## What good software does

- Single member database with renewal status, contact details, age, achievements
- Online event booking (parents log in, book, pay)
- Achievement tracker per child (badge attempts, dates, examiners)
- Auto-reminder for renewals, tests, vaccinations
- Financial reporting in standard PCAA format

## Specific UK Pony Club requirements

- **Safeguarding**: every adult helper requires DBS check, recorded with expiry
- **Test results**: must align with PC achievement levels and feed into national records
- **Camp regulations**: night supervision ratios, activity risk assessments, medical forms
- **Insurance**: PC blanket insurance applies to members at recognized events; non-member guests need separate cover
