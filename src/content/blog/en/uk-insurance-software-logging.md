UK riding establishments insure with specialists: **BETA Trade**, **BGA**, **South Essex Insurance**, **NFU Mutual**. Renewal questionnaires increasingly demand **digital records** as proof. If you can't produce them in 24 hours, premiums go up or coverage gets restricted.

## What insurers ask for at renewal

### Risk register

How many incidents in the last 12 months, by type:
- Falls (with injury / without)
- Kicks
- Equipment failures (girth break, bridle failure)
- Vehicle / horsebox incidents
- Slips, trips, falls (yard surfaces)

### Lesson volumes

- Total lesson hours
- Number of unique riders by age band (under 12, 12-17, 18+)
- Children-only lesson hours (separate insurance band)

### Hacking volume

- Number of hacks per month
- Average rider numbers per hack
- Routes used (mapped roads vs off-road bridleways)

### Staff records

- Number of qualified instructors (BHS / UKCC level)
- First aid certificate validity
- Safeguarding training currency

### Special activities

- Cross-country / hunting use of lesson horses
- Side-saddle, polo, trick riding
- Off-yard events (clinics, displays)

## What good software automatically tracks

- **Incident log**: every reported incident with date, time, severity, follow-up
- **Lesson volume**: aggregated by month, by instructor, by age band
- **Hack tracking**: who, when, where (route), duration, riders
- **Staff certifications**: with auto-expiry alerts
- **Equipment register**: helmets (replacement schedule), saddles, bridles with last fit-check

## The 3 common gaps

### "I'll remember to log it"

The kick on Tuesday afternoon doesn't get logged. Renewal questionnaire 6 months later misses it. **Insurance disputes a claim** when an incident isn't on file.

### Helmet replacement tracking

Schools should replace helmets every 5 years (or after impact). Lost helmets, damaged ones used "just for now" — this is the kind of thing that disqualifies a claim.

### Off-yard events

Clinic at someone else's yard, charity ride, demo at a county show. Often not insured under the standard policy. Tracking these separately and flagging insurance status before they happen avoids exposure.
